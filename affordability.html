What is the 28/36 rule?

Lenders generally cap your front-end ratio — housing costs (mortgage principal & interest, property tax, insurance, and HOA) — at about 28% of your gross monthly income, and your back-end ratio — all debt payments including the mortgage — at about 36%. Whichever limit is lower sets your budget. This calculator solves for the highest home price that keeps you inside both.
